01 — The gross web

Every invoice is a wire
waiting to happen.

Five subsidiaries. Twenty intercompany invoices. Three currencies. Settled bilaterally, that's twenty cross-border payments of FX spread, fees and float — every single month.

02 — Private by protocol

The others' flows aren't hidden.
They don't exist.

Canton discloses a contract only to its signatories and observers. Acting as the UK subsidiary, the ledger holds its eight invoices — the other twelve aren't filtered out. They were never sent.

03 — The collapse

Twenty obligations.
Three payments.

AtomicNet computes every subsidiary's net position and routes the minimum set of transfers. Singapore owed exactly as much as it was owed — so for Singapore, no money moves at all.

Σ NET POSITIONS = $0.00 · CONSERVED, ON-LEDGER

04 — Atomic

One transaction.
All or nothing.

Every leg settles inside a single Canton transaction — pre-authorized by each payer's co-signed allocation, checked for conservation on-ledger, reverted entirely if any leg fails. No partial settlement. No exposure.
